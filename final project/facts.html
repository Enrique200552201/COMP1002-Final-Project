Earth

Earth is about 4.54 billion years old.

Earth is approximately 510.1 million square kilometers.

About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water.

Earth rotates every 24 hours, creating day and night.

Mars

Mars is about 4.6 billion years old.

Mars has a diameter of approximately 6,779 kilometers.

Mars has a thin atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide.

Mars has two moons, Phobos and Deimos.

Venus

Venus is about 4.5 billion years old.

Venus has a diameter of approximately 12,104 kilometers.

Venus has a thick atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide.

Venus is often called Earth's "sister planet" due to their similar size and composition.

Jupiter

Jupiter is about 4.6 billion years old.

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with a diameter of approximately 139,822 kilometers.

Jupiter has a thick atmosphere primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.

Jupiter has a strong magnetic field and more than 75 moons, including the four largest known as the Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.

Saturn

Saturn is about 4.5 billion years old.

Saturn has a diameter of approximately 116,460 kilometers.

Saturn is known for its distinctive rings, which are primarily made up of ice particles and debris.

Saturn has more than 80 moons, with its largest moon, Titan, being larger than the planet Mercury.

Uranus

Uranus is about 4.5 billion years old.

Uranus has a diameter of approximately 50,724 kilometers.

Uranus is unique among the planets in our solar system because it rotates on its side.

Uranus has a thin ring system and 27 known moons.

Neptune

Neptune is about 4.5 billion years old.

Neptune has a diameter of approximately 49,244 kilometers.

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun in our solar system.

Neptune's atmosphere is primarily composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane.

Ceres

Ceres is about 4.6 billion years old.

Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.

Ceres has a diameter of approximately 940 kilometers.

Ceres is considered a dwarf planet and is the only dwarf planet located in the inner solar system.

Pluto

Pluto is about 4.6 billion years old.

Pluto was considered the ninth planet in our solar system until it was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006.

Pluto has a diameter of approximately 2,377 kilometers.

Pluto has a thin atmosphere primarily composed of nitrogen, methane, and carbon monoxide.

Eris

Eris is about 4.6 billion years old.

Eris is one of the largest known dwarf planets in our solar system.

Eris has a diameter of approximately 2,326 kilometers.

Eris was discovered in 2005 and is located in the scattered disc, a distant region of the solar system.

Sun

The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old.

The Sun has a diameter of approximately 1.39 million kilometers.

The Sun is a main-sequence star, and its energy is generated through nuclear fusion reactions in its core.

Makemake

Makemake is about 4.5 billion years old.

Makemake has a diameter of approximately 1,434 kilometers.

Makemake is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper belt.

Haumea

Haumea is about 4.5 billion years old.

Haumea has a diameter of approximately 1,960 kilometers.

Haumea is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper belt.

Moon

The Moon is about 4.5 billion years old.

The Moon has a diameter of approximately 3,474 kilometers.

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite.
